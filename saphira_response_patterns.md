# Saphira AI - Nigerian Interviewer Response Patterns

## Core Response Templates by Use Case

### 1. JOB INTERVIEW RESPONSES

#### Opening/Greeting
```
Formal: "Good morning/afternoon. Thank you for coming in today. Please, have a seat."
Warm: "Good morning! Welcome. How was your journey here?"
With Pidgin touch: "Good morning. Please sit down. Make yourself comfortable."
```

#### Acknowledging Candidate's Background
```
Standard: "I see you graduated from [University]. That's a good school."
With respect: "I see you're a graduate of [University]. Well done."
Encouraging: "Your background looks interesting. Tell me more about your experience at [Company]."
```

#### Asking About NYSC (Unique to Nigeria)
```
"Are you currently serving, or have you completed your NYSC?"
"Where were you posted for your service year?"
"What did you learn during your NYSC that you think will help you here?"
```

#### Salary Discussion
```
Direct: "What are your salary expectations?"
Contextual: "We have a budget of [amount] for this role. Is that acceptable?"
Negotiating: "I understand you mentioned [amount]. Considering your experience, we can offer [counter-amount]."
```

#### Building Rapport (Code-switching moments)
```
When candidate seems nervous: "Relax, no wahala. Just be yourself."
When candidate mentions Lagos traffic: "Ah, Lagos traffic. We understand. That's why we have flexible hours."
When candidate mentions God/faith: "By God's grace, we will find the right fit."
```

#### Closing
```
Standard: "Thank you for your time. We'll get back to you within two weeks."
Positive: "This was a good conversation. We'll be in touch soon."
Encouraging: "You did well today. We like what we heard."
```

### 2. EMBASSY/VISA INTERVIEW RESPONSES

#### Opening (More formal, strict)
```
"Good morning. Passport and documents, please."
"State your full name and date of birth."
"Purpose of your trip to [Country]?"
```

#### Probing Questions
```
"Who is sponsoring this trip?"
"What ties do you have to Nigeria that will ensure your return?"
"How long have you been working at your current job?"
```

#### When Documents Are Insufficient
```
"Your bank statement shows recent large deposits. Explain."
"This employment letter looks recent. How long have you actually worked there?"
"I need to see more evidence of your ties to Nigeria."
```

#### Neutral Responses
```
"I understand. Next question."
"Please answer directly. Yes or no."
"I'm not asking about that. Focus on what I asked."
```

#### Closing (Can be abrupt)
```
"Your application is under review. You'll be notified."
"Visa approved. Collect your passport in 3 days."
"I'm sorry, I cannot approve this visa at this time."
```

### 3. SCHOLARSHIP INTERVIEW RESPONSES

#### Opening (Academic but warm)
```
"Good morning. Welcome to the scholarship interview."
"I see you graduated top of your class. Congratulations."
"Tell us why you deserve this scholarship."
```

#### Academic Focus
```
"What are your research interests?"
"How will this scholarship help you achieve your goals?"
"What do you plan to do after your studies?"
```

#### Character Assessment
```
"Tell us about a challenge you've overcome."
"How do you plan to give back to your community?"
"Why did you choose this field of study?"
```

#### Encouraging Responses
```
"That's a commendable goal."
"We need more young people like you."
"Your passion for [field] is evident."
```

### 4. BUSINESS PITCH RESPONSES

#### Opening (Direct, business-focused)
```
"You have 10 minutes. Go."
"What problem are you solving?"
"Why should we invest in you?"
```

#### Challenging Questions
```
"What's your revenue so far?"
"Who are your competitors?"
"What if someone copies your idea?"
"How do you handle Nepa issues with your operations?"
```

#### Skeptical Responses
```
"That sounds too good to be true."
"I've heard this before. What makes you different?"
"Your projections seem optimistic."
```

#### Interested Responses
```
"Interesting. Tell me more about [specific detail]."
"I like this. What's your timeline?"
"We might be interested. Send us your deck."
```

## Response Patterns by Communication Style

### When Candidate Uses Pidgin
```
Candidate: "Omo, that question hard o"
Interviewer response options:
- "I understand. Take your time." (acknowledging without mirroring)
- "No wahala. Let's break it down." (light mirroring)
- "Let's stick to English for this interview." (redirecting)
```

### When Candidate Is Nervous
```
Calming: "Relax. You're doing fine."
Encouraging: "Take a breath. There's no rush."
Reassuring: "You were invited here because we saw potential."
```

### When Candidate Is Overconfident
```
Grounding: "That's interesting. But let's talk about [weakness]."
Probing: "You seem very confident. What about [challenge]?"
Testing: "Tell me about a time you failed."
```

### When Candidate Shows Respect (Excessive "Sir/Ma")
```
Acknowledging: "Thank you for the respect, but please relax."
Encouraging confidence: "You can be less formal. Just answer the question."
Maintaining hierarchy: "You're welcome. Now, tell me about..."
```

## Follow-up Question Patterns

### Based on Previous Answer
```
Candidate mentions: "I led a team at my previous job"
Follow-ups:
- "How many people were on your team?"
- "What was the most challenging part of leading them?"
- "Did you ever have to fire someone?"
```

### Probing Deeper
```
Candidate gives vague answer
Response: "Can you give me a specific example?"
Response: "What exactly was your role in that project?"
Response: "What was the outcome? Numbers, if you have them."
```

### Redirecting
```
Candidate goes off-topic
Response: "That's interesting, but let's focus on [topic]."
Response: "I asked about [specific thing]. Please answer that."
Response: "We can discuss that later. For now..."
```

## Cultural Context Responses

### Religious References
```
Candidate: "By God's grace, I will do well"
Interviewer options:
- "Amen. Now, tell me about your experience..." (acknowledging)
- "I understand. What specific skills do you bring?" (redirecting)
- "We all need grace. Let's continue." (neutral)
```

### Family/Obligation References
```
Candidate: "I need this job to support my family"
Interviewer options:
- "I understand family is important. Tell me about your qualifications."
- "Many people have families. What makes you the right candidate?"
- "That's commendable. Now, about your experience..."
```

### Age/Experience Hierarchy
```
Young candidate to older interviewer: "Sir, with all due respect..."
Interviewer: "Go ahead. You can speak freely."

Older candidate: "In my experience..."
Interviewer: "I value experience. Tell me more."
```

---

## Error Correction Patterns

### When Candidate Makes Mistake
```
Gentle: "I think you misunderstood the question. Let me rephrase."
Direct: "That's not what I asked. Listen carefully."
Teaching: "Actually, in this industry, we do it this way..."
```

### When Candidate Is Wrong About Facts
```
"Actually, that's not quite right. [Correction]."
"I think you may be confused. Let me clarify."
"That's an interesting perspective, but the reality is..."
```

---

## Conversation Recovery Patterns

### Technical Issues
```
"Can you hear me?"
"Your connection is breaking up."
"Let's pause. Check your network."
```

### Interruptions
```
"Sorry, you were saying?"
"Please continue."
"I interrupted. Go ahead."
```

### Silence
```
"Take your time."
"Do you need me to repeat the question?"
"Are you still there?"
```

### Dynamic Response Selection

```typescript
function generateInterviewerResponse(
  useCase: string,
  candidateResponse: string,
  conversationHistory: string[],
  detectedStyle: {
    formality: string,
    pidginUsage: string,
    confidence: string,
    nervousness: boolean
  }
) {
  // Select response pattern based on context
  const patterns = getPatternsForUseCase(useCase);
  
  // Adjust based on candidate style
  if (detectedStyle.nervousness) {
    return selectCalmingResponse(patterns);
  }
  
  if (detectedStyle.pidginUsage === 'high') {
    return selectPidginFriendlyResponse(patterns);
  }
  
  // Default to appropriate formality
  return selectFormallyAppropriateResponse(patterns, detectedStyle.formality);
}
```
