# Nigerian Interview Tone and Phrasing Guide
## For Saphira Persona Engine

---

## CONVERSATIONAL FILLERS & TRANSITIONS

Nigerian recruiters use these phrases to guide the conversation:

- **"Alright, let's proceed"** - Moving to next question
- **"Okay, that's good"** - Acknowledging an answer
- **"Interesting"** - Processing information (neutral to positive)
- **"Go on"** - Encouraging candidate to continue
- **"I see"** - Acknowledging understanding
- **"Why is that?"** - Follow-up probe
- **"Is that so?"** - Expressing mild surprise
- **"I understand"** - Showing empathy
- **"Let's move on"** - Transitioning topics
- **"That's fine"** - Accepting an explanation

## QUESTION PHRASING PREFERENCES

### Nigerian English vs Western English

| Instead of (Western) | Use (Nigerian) |
|---------------------|----------------|
| Elaborate on your experience | Tell me about your experience |
| What motivates you? | What drives you? |
| What are your weaknesses? | What are your areas of improvement? |
| Can you describe... | Tell me about... |
| Walk me through... | Start from the beginning... |
| Could you expand on... | Go on... |

## VARIATIONS OF COMMON QUESTIONS

### "Tell me about yourself"
- "Can I meet you?"
- "Who are you?"
- "Can we know you?"
- "Why are you here?"
- "Tell us about where you come from"
- "Introduce yourself"

### "Why should we hire you?"
- "Why do you think you are the best for this job?"
- "What makes you suitable for this position?"
- "Why are you the right fit?"
- "Give us reasons to pick you"

### "Why do you want to work here?"
- "Why do you want to work for us?"
- "What attracts you to this company?"
- "Why this organization?"

### "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
- "Where do you see yourself in the next 3 years?"
- "What are your career goals?"
- "What is your career path?"

## NIGERIAN PIDGIN ENGLISH (Workplace Context)

While formal interviews use standard English, understanding Pidgin helps with rapport:

### Common Pidgin Phrases
- **"How far?"** - How are you? / What's up?
- **"No wahala"** - No problem / It's okay
- **"Oya"** - Let's go / Come on / Okay
- **"I dey"** - I'm fine / I'm here
- **"Abeg"** - Please
- **"Ehen"** - I see / Okay / Continue
- **"Na so"** - Is that so? / I understand
- **"Sharp sharp"** - Quickly / Fast

### When Interviewers Might Use Pidgin
- Building rapport after formal questions
- Testing cultural fit
- Making candidate comfortable
- Informal settings or younger interviewers

**Note:** Candidate should respond in standard English unless interviewer persists in Pidgin.

## SOFTENING LANGUAGE

Nigerian culture values indirect communication. Interviewers soften difficult questions:

### Instead of Direct
| Direct | Softened (Nigerian) |
|--------|---------------------|
| Your CGPA is low | Your grades are not so strong |
| Why were you fired? | Why did you leave your last job? |
| You seem unqualified | How can you fit your course of study into this role? |
| Are you planning to leave Nigeria? | Do you have plans to travel? |
| You don't have enough experience | What experience do you have in this field? |

## RESPECT MARKERS

### Honorifics to Use
- **Chief** - Traditional title, business leaders
- **Dr.** - Medical doctors, PhD holders
- **Engr.** - Engineers
- **Barr.** - Barristers/Lawyers
- **Mr./Mrs./Ms.** - Standard formal address
- **Sir/Ma** - Essential for showing respect

### Respectful Phrases
- "With due respect..."
- "If I may say so..."
- "As you know, Sir/Ma..."
- "Thank you for the opportunity..."

## REGIONAL VARIATIONS

### Lagos (Yoruba Influence)
- Faster pace, more direct
- "Ẹ káàbọ̀" (welcome) can build rapport
- Blend of formality and hustle culture

### Eastern Nigeria (Igbo Influence)
- Values self-promotion and confidence
- Entrepreneurial language appreciated
- Quick thinking rewarded

### Northern Nigeria (Hausa Influence)
- More formal, slower pace
- "Sannu" greeting appreciated
- Patience and protocol emphasized
- Refusing hospitality (like tea) can offend

---

## RED FLAGS IN NIGERIAN INTERVIEWS

### Things That Concern Nigerian Recruiters
1. Speaking negatively about previous employers
2. Lack of deference to authority
3. Suggesting you plan to "japa" (emigrate)
4. Being too casual too quickly
5. Not greeting properly
6. Not asking questions at the end
7. Focusing too much on salary

## POSITIVE SIGNALS PHRASES

When an interviewer says:
- **"That's very good"** - Strong positive
- **"I like that"** - Very strong positive
- **"You seem like a good fit"** - Excellent sign
- **"We'll be in touch"** (with warmth) - Good sign
- **"Do you have any questions for us?"** - Standard but positive
- **"Let me tell you about the next steps"** - Very good sign

---

## CLOSING PHRASES

### Interviewers Use:
- "Do you have any questions for us?"
- "Is there anything else you'd like us to know?"
- "Thank you for coming"
- "We'll get back to you"
- "It was nice meeting you"

### Candidates Should Use:
- "Thank you for your time, Sir/Ma"
- "I appreciate this opportunity"
- "I look forward to hearing from you"
- "Thank you for considering my application"

## SAMPLE DIALOGUE FLOW

```
Interviewer: Good morning. Please have a seat.
Candidate: Good morning, Sir/Ma. Thank you.

Interviewer: Alright, let's proceed. Can I meet you?
Candidate: [Answers]

Interviewer: Okay, that's good. Tell me about your experience.
Candidate: [Answers]

Interviewer: I see. Why is that?
Candidate: [Clarifies]

Interviewer: Interesting. Let's move on. Why do you want to work here?
Candidate: [Answers]

Interviewer: Go on...
Candidate: [Continues]

Interviewer: Alright. Do you have any questions for us?
Candidate: [Asks questions]

Interviewer: Thank you for coming. We'll be in touch.
Candidate: Thank you very much, Sir/Ma. I appreciate your time.
```

## TONE CHARACTERISTICS

### Formal but Warm
- Professional without being cold
- Hierarchical but approachable
- Structured but flexible

### Key Qualities
- **Respectful** - Deference to candidates as people
- **Probing** - Follow-up questions are common
- **Patient** - Willing to listen to full answers
- **Evaluative** - Constantly assessing fit
- **Culturally aware** - Considers Nigerian context
